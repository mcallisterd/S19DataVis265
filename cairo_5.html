Making Something Great

  1. Visualization is effective primarily thanks to how powerful our eyes are and how clear certain relationships can be made thanks to a well- crafted visualization which takes a complex or large set of data and condenses it into a comprehensible set of charts or other ideas. By organizing data or grouping informaiton, trends, patterns, and relationships are much more easily grasped. In part this is a dangerous thing, as information can be presented in a misleading way that supports a potentially biased viewpoint or argument.
  2. Depending on your goal with a particular visualization, there is some variation in what type of chart is best. When specific, numerical relationships are meant to be conveyed, line graphs, bar charts, and "lollipop charts" which are aligned along some common scale, with a reasonable baseline, often 0 but perhaps something different based on context, are the best way to convey a numerical relationship. Other more general trends, like groupings based on type or class, that use color or area, can be effective in illustrating how certain large groups of objects relate, but are less precise, so are best used to merely convey general trends as I prefaced.
  3. Cairo mentions that hard and fast rules for what visualization is right for what situation essentially don't exist, and that there are certain charts and graphs which are perhaps right for one situation while broadly inappropriate for other tasks. His example with a radar chart to illustrate how voting in a Brazilian election unfolded based on geographical region is apt, where comparing multiple radar charts on the same axes to discern which baseball player, again his example, is better performing along certain measures, is by comparison inappropriate unless you mean to make interpretting the chart difficult. Again, you choose which chart to use based on circumstance: there is no one chart that is always bad or one chart that will always yield an excellent visualization, and developing a sense for which chart is effective for which task allows one to be flexible in designing visualizations to maximize communicative power and not overlook promising options.
